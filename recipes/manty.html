Manty recipe (version with Pumpkin)

Description

Manty are a beloved traditional Qazaq dish consisting of steamed dumplings filled with seasoned meat and onions. They are considered one of the most hearty and
iconic foods of Qazaq cuisine. Manty are a staple of Qazaq household cooking and symbolize hospitality and abundance. Because they're easy to prepare in large quantities,
they're often associated with family feasts and special occasions.

Ingridients

  • Meat - 500 g
  • Onion - 3 pcs
  • Salt - to taste
  • Pepper - to taste
  • Flour - 300 g
  • Pumpkin - 300 g
  • Milk - 500 ml
  • Margarine - 100 g
  • Egg - 1 pc
  • Sugar - 1 tbs
  • Yeast - 30 g

Steps

  1. Dissolve the yeast in the milk, add the salt, sugar, softened margarine, egg, and flour, and knead the dough. Cover it with a damp cloth and leave it for 40 minutes.
  2. Add finely chopped onion, grated pumpkin (using a coarse grater), salt, and pepper to the meat passed through a meat grinder, and mix thoroughly.
  3. Roll the dough into thin ropes, cut into pieces weighing 20 g each, and roll each piece into a small circle. Place some filling in the center and pinch the edges of the dough upward so it resembles a small onion.
  4. Place the manty on greased steam-tray tiers (kaskans), set them over a pot of boiling water, and steam for 40 minutes. Brush the cooked manty with butter.
